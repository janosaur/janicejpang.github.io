Delivering the helpfulness of Google in retail

In 2018, my team at Grow partnered with Google to create the global in-store demo for their flagship smartphone, the Pixel 3 and 3XL.

Our demo experience showcased the phone’s incredible features through a self-guided tour. The demo allowed customers to easily jump from learning about a feature to trying it, showing first-time Pixel users how easy it is to make the switch. We localized the demo experience for 11 markets – all in time for the phone's global launch.

Understanding the retail environment

To create an effective demo, we needed to know what kind of environment and mindset customers would be in when using it. We conducted 3 studies to identify insights about the retail environment, its smartphone offerings, and its customers. Here's what we learned from each analysis:

Analysis 1

Retail Reconnaissance

We conducted 10 mystery shopping studies at Best Buy and Verizon stores across the country to see first-hand what the smartphone retail experience looked, sounded, and felt like.

Analysis 1: Findings

  • Big players dominate. Large signage and backlit displays sectioned off Apple and Samsung's territories, each of which housed multiple smartphone options. In comparison, each store had just a 1 or 2 Pixels on display.
  • The humans aren’t very helpful. Pixel information communicated by the Retail Sales Associates varied widely; at best, it was incomplete - often it was incorrect. When a shopper was interested in a Pixel, the RSA would steer the conversation away from Pixel to a different phone.

Analysis 2

Product Comparisons

We audited online product reviews of competitor phones from both industry experts and consumers. We wanted to understand what features excited customers, and what they found lacking.
