Make the complex simple.

Simplify

Our lives are shaped by decisions – some of which may be as inconsequential as what we'll eat for lunch; others as influential as where we'll go (aka what we'll do) post-college.

In an attempt to make the complex simple., I prototyped a decision-making web application called Simplify. This project attempts to dismantle and provide transparency to the nuanced thought processes behind human decisions.

For me, the challenge in creating this project was to slow myself down – to take the the time and actually think about my own thinking. To make the complex simple, I've learned, requires a holistic understanding of what makes something so complex – and the ability to identify the steps necessary to pare it down.

→ Explore the desktop prototype!

Following the principles of multiple-criteria decision analysis, this web app walks the user through process of accounting for decision factors and assessing those factors' relevance.

While the example decision prototyped is one of little consequence, the same questions and considerations can be applied to decisions of any gravity.
