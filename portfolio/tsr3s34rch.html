Bringing awareness to Google's best-kept secret.


Billions of people trust Google's products to help them every day — that’s why helpfulness is at the core of their mission. Being helpful also means being responsible and preventing harm for their users and for society as a whole. That’s where the Google Trust & Safety team comes in.

The Trust & Safety team produces critical research that informs policies on online issues like fraud, spam, and abuse. This research has profound impact within and beyond Google, yet the team behind it suffers from being unknown.

My team worked closely with the Trust & Safety team to create a digital platform that brings the team and their research to light, extending the reach and impact of their work.

The Process

Stakeholder interviews

Before diving into the content, we needed to learn more about who we were going to be designing for. We interviewed 9 stakeholders to familiarize ourselves with the team, their research, and their audience. Here's what we learned:

Finding 1

The Trust & Safety research team has 3 key audiences. We define these audiences by how they interact with the research.

  1. Super Users
    • T & S researchers within Google
    • Other Google researchers
    • Researchers in academia, non-profits, and think tanks

  2. Engaged Users
    • Google product & policy teams
    • Tech industry T & S teams

  3. Occasional Users
    • Google marketers
    • Government officials
    • Media organizations

Finding 2

External and internal audiences struggle to describe the mission and the scope of Trust & Safety Research. This confusion is a barrier to engagement.

Finding 3

The current research categorizations and track names aren’t working as hard as they could to improve understanding across the board, nor were they designed to.

Finding 4

Giving your audiences access to the research doesn’t inherently translate to understanding and application.
