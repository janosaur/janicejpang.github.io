LitMix

LitMix is a new side project which pairs literature & music.

Mitchell Zukor – mathematician and protagonist of Nathaniel Rich’s dystopian disaster novel Odds Against Tomorrow – profits from fear. More specifically, fear of the future; of natural disasters, incurable diseases, nuclear wars; of their inevitability and imminence. As an insurance consultant, Mitchell sells his clients on the likelihood of worst-case scenarios.

The best, or perhaps worst, part is Mitchell himself is completely convinced of his calculations. When one of his predictions proves true, Mitchell must overcome his own fear of fear to survive. The flooding of New York City – fantastically imagined by Oliver Munday – becomes the shore on which human character, civility, and faith ebbs and flows.

LitMix traces Mitchell’s journey from modern man to modern prophet; his relationships with not one but two manic-pixie-dream-girls; and the casual, calculated degradation of planet Earth. This compilation is best enjoyed with “the right mix of technical knowledge and personal despair.”
