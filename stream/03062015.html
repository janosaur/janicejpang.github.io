I've lost a lot of creativity in 2 years.

Looking at my blog posts from the summer between my Freshman and Sophomore year of college, I realize I haven't let myself experiment in that way in a long time. That summer, I took an art class at a local community college. For three hours every Monday, Wednesday, and Thursday, I and 14 other students sat in a stuffy studio and made things. The assignments were a mix of intro art class repetitiveness ("Draw a ball from 15 different angles!") and more advanced, abstract challenges, like the paper cutout below.

An early assignment from my art class. Damn, look at that CRAFTSMANSHIP (I joke). See more paper cutouts on my Tumblr.

When I think about what gave me the sense of creative freedom I now feel I lack – it was time. I had time to experiment; to start something from scratch if I didn't like it; to play.
