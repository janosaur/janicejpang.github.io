03.03.2015 – 10:08 PM


I've lost a lot of creativity in 2 years.

Looking at my blog posts from the summer between my Freshman and Sophomore year of college, I realize I haven't let myself experiment in that way in a long time. That summer, I took an art class at a local community college. For three hours every Monday, Wednesday, and Thursday, I and 14 other students sat in a stuffy studio and made things. The assignments were a mix of intro art class repetitiveness ("Draw a ball from 15 different angles!") and more abstract challenges like the paper cutout below.

An early assignment from my art class. Damn, look at that CRAFTSMANSHIP (I joke). See more paper cutouts on my Tumblr.

Two years ago, I had sense of creative freedom I now feel I lack; I attribute that freedom to time. I had time then – time uninterrupted by obligations of classes, two jobs, etc. – to experiment; to start something from scratch if I didn't like it; to play.

Perhaps I'm quicker at making things I can be proud of – or perhaps I really have lost that creative spark and am simply following UX/UI patterns – but something about working in that stuffy studio or in that stuffy room, and working to just try something new, felt so much more rewarding.

As I mentioned in my last blog post, I've been feeling pretty overwhelmed.

Last Wednesday, over ddukbokki and hot tea, I told my parents my plans to take Spring Quarter off, or at least part-time. When they asked why, I explained how, since Fall Quarter, stress had been building, building, building. I think it was the first time I'd ever admitted to my parents any feelings of stress or anxiety.

Neither of them spoke for a few seconds – unusual during our meals – looking at me.

"Thank you for telling us," my dad said.

I'm not sure what kind of reaction I was expecting from them, but my dad's words were relieving. I felt validated in my decision to let myself take a break next quarter – to let myself focus on two or three things (instead of six or seven) and really dedicate myself to them.

Triadic color scheme collage. I remember having a lot of fun with this project. See more of this collage on my Tumblr.

Next quarter, I'm looking forward to having a lot more time. I'm still battling with whether I feel like I'm giving in or giving up, but I'm taking fact that I feel a huge weight lifted off me as a good sign.
