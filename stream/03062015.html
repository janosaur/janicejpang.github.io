03.06.2015 – 4:40 AM


I've lost a lot of creativity in 2 years.

Looking at my blog posts from the summer between my Freshman and Sophomore year of college, I realize I haven't let myself experiment in that way in a long time. That summer, I took an art class at a local community college. For three hours every Monday, Wednesday, and Thursday, I and 14 other students sat in a stuffy studio and made things. The assignments were a mix of intro art class repetitiveness ("Draw a ball from 15 different angles!") and more abstract challenges like the paper cutout below.

An early assignment from my art class. Damn, look at that CRAFTSMANSHIP (I joke). See more paper cutouts on my Tumblr.

Two years ago, I had sense of creative freedom I now feel I lack; I attribute that freedom to time. I had time then – time uninterrupted by obligations of classes, two jobs, etc. – to experiment; to start something from scratch if I didn't like it; to play.

Perhaps I'm simply more efficient at making things I can be proud of than I was two years ago, and am thus required to do more to achieve that same feeling. Something about working in that stuffy studio, though, felt so much more rewarding.

As I wrote in my last blog post, these past 6 months have been overwhelming for me. They've been rewarding, sure, but they've also left little time for personal growth or care.

This past Wednesday, over ddukbokki and hot tea, I told my parents my plans to take Spring Quarter off, or at least part-time. When they asked why, I explained how, since Fall Quarter, stress had been building, building, building. This was the first time I'd ever admitted to my parents any sentiment of not being okay.

Neither of them spoke for a few seconds – unusual during our meals – their brows slightly furrowed.

"Thank you for telling us," my dad said, emphasizing you and us. His brows softened.

I'm not sure what kind of reaction I was expecting from them, but my dad's words were relieving. I felt validated in my decision to let myself take a break – to focus on two or three things (instead of six or seven) and really dedicate myself to them.

Triadic color scheme collage. I remember having a lot of fun with this project. See more of this collage on my Tumblr.

Next quarter, I'm looking forward to having more time. What I'll do with that time I'm still figuring out. I'm still battling with whether I feel like I'm giving in / up, but I'm taking fact that I feel emotionally lighter & mentally clearer as a good sign.

If you've read this far, please take this song and it's lovely forest sounds as my thank you:


I believe in doing things that make me whole
Not incomplete 'cause in the grander scheme of things
Who am I if I'm not happy?
